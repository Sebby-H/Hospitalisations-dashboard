About this Dashboard

This dashboard presents hospitalised injuries in Australia where the cause was a road crash, covering the period from 2011 to 2021. Data is sourced from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE) and processed using KNIME Analytics Platform. The visualisations were built using D3.js as part of the COS30045 Data Visualisation project at Swinburne University of Technology.


The Data Dictionary, available via the data source button below, identifies the specific hospitalisation cause codes which have been reported. Raw counts are used throughout. Direct comparison across states or population groups should account for differences in population size, as noted in the Data Limitations section.

Key Definitions

Hospitalised injuries
A hospitalised injury is defined as an injury which results in the person being admitted to hospital, and subsequently discharged alive either on the same day or after one or more nights stay in a hospital bed (i.e. deaths are excluded). Transfers between hospitals are only counted once.

The circumstances of the injury are coded using the International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems Australian Modification (ICD-10-AM) classification system.
Sex
The sex of the road user contains the category "unknown". This also includes cases where the sex was intersex or indeterminate. Unknown values are excluded from the gender filter in this dashboard.
Age group
Age groups are defined as: 0–7, 8–16, 17–25, 26–39, 40–64, 65–74, and 75 and over. Unknown or missing age group values are excluded from age-related charts.

Road Users

The road user type refers to the injured person at the time of injury. It is determined through the ICD-10-AM coding of external cause of injury. In some cases a vehicle occupant cannot be determined and is coded as "Driver/Passenger".

Data Limitations

Raw counts are used throughout this dashboard. The following limitations should be considered when interpreting the data:
Population differences across states and age groups are not accounted for. Larger states will naturally show higher raw counts.
Unknown or missing values for sex, age group, and road user type are excluded from filtered views.
Data is subject to revision. Refer also to disclaimers at infrastructure.gov.au/disclaimers.
